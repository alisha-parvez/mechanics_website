Concept for ex 1

The first step in such questions is to find the reaction forces at the hinge. A hinge is similar to a pin in the sense that it can exert forces along the x and y directions but it cannot exert a balancing moment.
In order to solve such questions, we divide the beam into 2 parts, along the hinge, and treat each part as an independent body.
Then, on applying the equilibrium reactions for each part, we can find the values of the hinge reactions.

We divide the beam into two parts, along the hinge
For each part, we assume that the hinge exerts reactions Gx and Gy along the x and y directions respectively. In order to keep the same FBD for the original beam on superposing the two parts, the directions of these forces on the two parts must be opposite.

For the part on the right of the hinge:

Applying Fx = 0 , we get:
Gx - 10 = 0 => Gx = 10 kN

Applying Fy = 0 , we get:
- 5*4 + Gy + Rb - 5 = 0 => Gy + Rb = 25 kN

Applying Mz about G = 0 , we get:
- 5*4*2 + Rb*4 - 5*5 = 0 => Rb = 16.25 kN

Thus, Gy = 25 - Rb = 8.75 kN


For the part on the left of the hinge:

Applying Fx = 0 , we get:
RAX - 10 = 0 => RAX = 10 kN

Applying Fy = 0 , we get:
RAY - 10 - 8.75 = 0 => RAY = 18.75 kN

Applying Mz = 0 about A, we get:
MA + 10*2 + 8.75*4 = 0 =>MA = - 55 kNm
