Concept for ex 3

Distributed forces are those forces that act on a large part of a surface, not just on one point.
In two dimesions, the intensity of a distributed force is the force per unit length. The intensity may vary or be constant
For purposes of analysis, the distributed force can be considered as a single force whose magnitude is the integral or summation of the length element times the intensity for that elemnent i.e. (i)(dl)
It can be calculated as the 'area' of the figure of distributed force.
The force is considered to act at the centroid of the distributed force figure
E.g. For constant intensity, the magnitude is the the length for which the force acts times the force intensity i.e. the length*breadth of the rectangular figure and the force acts at the centre
E.g. For a linearly increasing intensity, the calculation is shown:

  • Magnitude: (1/2) * (base) * (height) = (1/2) * (3 m) * (10 kN/m) = 15 kN
  • Distance from A : Centroid= (2/3) * (3 m) = 2 m
